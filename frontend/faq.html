Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about solar thermal physics and system diagnostics

What is the primary purpose of this platform?

This platform is an academic engineering tool designed to simulate Solar Thermal Systems, analyze their thermodynamic performance, and use AI-driven logic to diagnose common operational faults like Dust Accumulation, Heat Leakage, and Pump Failure.

How is Thermal Efficiency calculated?

Thermal Efficiency (η) is calculated as the ratio of Useful Heat Gain ($Q_{u}$) to the total Solar Energy hitting the collector area ($G \times A$):

η = (ṁ × Cp × (Tout - Tin)) / (G × A)

Where ṁ is mass flow rate, Cp is specific heat, and G is solar irradiance.

What triggers a "Dust Accumulation" fault?

This fault is triggered when the system detects that the actual outlet temperature is significantly lower than the theoretical ideal, despite high solar irradiance. This suggests the glass cover is partially blocked, preventing photons from reaching the absorber plate.

Why do I see different results in Light vs. Dark mode?

The underlying calculations and data remain identical! The theme change is purely visual to assist in different environments—Dark Mode is optimized for digital displays and reduced eye strain, while Light Mode provides high contrast for documentation and reports.

How can I export my simulation data?

Navigate to the "Results & Logs" page and click "Download Full Report". The system will generate a professional PDF containing your performance metrics, fault history, and maintenance suggestions for your current session.

What should I do if a "Pump Degradation" is detected?

Check the mass flow rate settings. In a real system, this indicates a mechanical blockage or scaling in the impeller. Consult the Maintenance Guide at the bottom of the "Fault Diagnosis" page for specific troubleshooting steps.

What does "Sensor Drift" indicate in the diagnosis?

Sensor Drift occurs when the AI detects physically impossible results, such as the water gaining more energy than is falling on the collector from the sun. This usually means the temperature sensors need recalibration or there is electrical interference in the measurement wires.

How is the "System Health Index" calculated?

The health index is a weighted score out of 100. It starts at 100 and drops based on the frequency and severity of detected faults. A score below 70 indicates that the system is significantly underperforming and requires a physical inspection.

Why is "Ambient Temperature" required for simulation?

Ambient temperature is critical for calculating Thermal Energy Loss. Heat loss to the surrounding air increases when the outside temperature is low, which directly impacts the collector's overall efficiency even if the sun is shining brightly.

Is my simulation data kept private from other users?

Yes! The platform uses "Session Isolation" based on your User ID. When you enter a name or ID, the system creates a dedicated secure folder on the backend. Your history, charts, and PDF reports are only accessible within your specific session.
